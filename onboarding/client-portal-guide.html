Your Dashboard Guide

The dashboard is the part that makes TaylorURL different. It's where you watch your site work — visitors arriving, pages doing well, the site staying up — in real time, without me in the middle.

1 Signing in

Once I create your account, you'll get an email invite to pick a password. From then on:

  1. Visit your dashboard address (I'll share this on kickoff).
  2. Enter your email and password.
  3. Land directly on your dashboard.

Forgot your password?

Use the "Forgot password" link on the sign-in screen. A reset email arrives within a minute. If it doesn't, check spam, then text me — I can resend it manually.

3 Reading the status badges

Status badges are the at-a-glance signal that something needs attention — or that everything's fine. You'll see them throughout the portal.

Healthy Degraded Down

What each one means

  • Healthy — site responding normally, uptime holding, no alerts firing.
  • Degraded — site is up but something is slower or off (a third-party API, an asset, a region). I'm aware and watching.
  • Down — site is unreachable. I'm notified the moment this changes; you should be too.

5 Inviting team members

You can bring teammates into the portal so they see the same numbers you do — without sharing your password.

  1. Open Settings → Team in the portal.
  2. Click Invite member.
  3. Enter their email address and choose the client role.
  4. They'll get an email invite to set their own password and sign in.

Need someone removed? Same screen, same role — and I'm happy to handle removals for you if that's easier.

6 Reading the metrics well

Dashboards lie when they're read poorly. A few habits that make this one useful:

  • Compare to last week, not yesterday. Day-to-day swings are noise; week-over-week tells the real story.
  • Watch which pages move. A visitor spike is interesting; a visitor spike on your service page is actionable.
  • Treat 99.9% uptime as the floor. If it slips below that for a week, ask why — I'll already be looking.
  • Don't panic on a single degraded badge. Sometimes a third party hiccups. Sustained degradation is the signal that matters.

If you ever can't tell what a number means, ask. The portal exists to give you clarity, not homework.
