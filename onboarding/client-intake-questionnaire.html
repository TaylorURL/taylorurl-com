Client Intake Questionnaire

The answers below are how I turn "let's build a site" into a clear, scoped, on-time project. Fill in what you can — we'll work through the rest together in the kickoff call.

1 Business goals & target audience

Before pixels, I need to understand why this site exists and who it's for.

What does your business do, in one sentence?Imagine explaining it to a neighbor who's never heard of you. What is the single most important outcome this site needs to drive?Calls, form fills, bookings, online orders, applications — pick one primary action. Who is the ideal visitor?Industry, role, location, budget range, the problem they're trying to solve. What problems does your business solve for them better than the alternatives? How will you know the site is working three months after launch?Specific numbers are great — "30 leads/month" beats "more traffic."

2 Your brand pieces

If it already exists, send it over. If it doesn't, I'll either make it or give you a clear plan for it.

Logo filesAny format you have. Send a version with a transparent background if you can. Dark and light versions both help. Brand colorsThe exact colors if you have them. Otherwise point me at something that feels right. FontsNames of any fonts you already use in your branding. Photos & imageryExisting photos, who took them, or a note that you'll need help getting some. How you want to soundThree words that describe the tone (e.g. confident, friendly, warm). Anything off-limits?Colors, words, images, or competitor styles you want to avoid.

3 Your web address, hosting & logins

I never touch things I don't have clear handoff for. Tell me what already exists and who has the keys.

Web address you wantThe address the new site will live at (yourbusiness.com), and whether you prefer it with or without "www" in front. Where is the web address registered?GoDaddy, Namecheap, Google Domains, etc. Note who has the login. Where is your current site hosted, if any?If you have an existing site, who runs it for you? Business email tied to this addressBusiness email running on this address (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, etc.) I should not break. Existing Google or traffic reportsGoogle Analytics, Google Search Console, Meta Pixel — anything I should hang on to. Social media pages to link to

4 Content & copy

Words are usually the longest lead time on a site. Tell me where the content stands.

Who is writing the copy?You, your team, me, or a mix? Existing content I should reuseOld site pages, brochures, sales decks, PDFs. Required legal pagesPrivacy policy, terms of service, accessibility statement. Testimonials or case studies available? Service area, hours, phone, email to display

5 Pages & features

A first pass at what pages and features you want. We'll firm this up together on the kickoff call.

Pages you know you needHome, Services, About, Contact, FAQ, Blog, Portfolio, Pricing, etc. Forms on the siteContact form, quote request, booking, newsletter signup — and where the messages should go. Tools to connectCustomer list, scheduling tool, payment processor, email marketing, calendar. Anything specialMembers area, locked content, multi-language, photo gallery, map, video. What you want to be found for on GoogleThe kinds of searches you want to show up in, and the towns you serve.

6 Competitors & references

Show me the field so I can design intentionally — not by accident.

Top three competitor websitesThe ones you'd hate to lose a customer to. Sites you love (in any industry)What about each one resonates? Layout, tone, photography, motion? Sites you can't standEqually useful — tells me what to avoid.

7 Timeline & budget

Be honest. A real number lets me recommend something that actually fits.

When do you want to be live?A specific date, a season, or "as soon as possible." Hard deadlinesTrade show, grand opening, end of quarter, anything else with a real date. Budget rangeA range is fine. Helps me decide what to include. What kind of ongoing help do you want?Hands-off after launch, monthly care plan, or somewhere in between? Who has to say yes?The people on your side who make the call, and the order they need to approve.
