Client Intake Questionnaire

The answers below are how I turn "let's build a site" into a clear, scoped, on-time project. Fill in what you can — we'll work through the rest together in the kickoff call.

1 Business goals & target audience

Before pixels, I need to understand why this site exists and who it's for.

What does your business do, in one sentence?Imagine explaining it to a neighbor who's never heard of you. What is the single most important outcome this site needs to drive?Calls, form fills, bookings, online orders, applications — pick one primary action. Who is the ideal visitor?Industry, role, location, budget range, the problem they're trying to solve. What problems does your business solve for them better than the alternatives? How will you know the site is working three months after launch?Specific numbers are great — "30 leads/month" beats "more traffic."

2 Brand assets

If it already exists, send it over. If it doesn't, I'll either create it or make a clear plan for it.

Logo filesSVG preferred. PNG with a transparent background is fine. Send dark and light versions if you have them. Brand colorsHex codes if you have them. Otherwise point us at something that feels right. FontsNames of typefaces you already use, or license info if they're paid fonts. Photography & imageryExisting photo library, photographer credits, or a note that you'll need help sourcing. Brand voice & toneThree adjectives that describe how you want to sound (e.g. confident, plain-spoken, warm). Anything off-limits?Colors, words, imagery, or competitor look-and-feels you want to avoid.

3 Existing domain, hosting & access

We never make changes to things we don't have a clear handoff for. List what exists and who controls it.

Primary domainThe domain the new site will live on, including any www / apex preferences. Where is the domain registered?GoDaddy, Namecheap, Google Domains, etc. Note who has the login. Current hosting provider, if anyIf you have an existing site, where is it hosted now? DNS / email considerationsBusiness email running on this domain (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, etc.) we should not disrupt. Existing analytics / trackingGoogle Analytics, Search Console, Meta Pixel, anything we should preserve. Existing social profiles to link to

4 Content & copy

Words are usually the longest lead time on a site. Tell us where the content stands.

Who is writing the copy?You, your team, us, or a mix? Existing content we should reuseOld site pages, brochures, sales decks, PDFs. Required legal pagesPrivacy policy, terms of service, accessibility statement. Testimonials or case studies available? Service area, hours, phone, email to display

5 Pages & features

A first pass at the sitemap and feature set. We will refine this in Discovery.

Pages you know you needHome, Services, About, Contact, FAQ, Blog, Portfolio, Pricing, etc. Forms & submissionsContact form, quote request, booking, newsletter signup — where do the submissions need to go? IntegrationsCRM, scheduling tool, payment processor, email marketing, calendar. Special featuresMembers area, gated content, multi-language, gallery, map, video. Search engine prioritiesKeywords you want to rank for, geographic targeting.

6 Competitors & references

Show us the field so we can design intentionally — not by accident.

Top three competitor websitesThe ones you'd hate to lose a customer to. Sites you love (in any industry)What about each one resonates? Layout, tone, photography, motion? Sites you can't standEqually useful — tells us what to avoid.

7 Timeline & budget

Be honest. A real number lets us recommend a realistic scope.

When would you like to launch?A specific date, a season, or "as soon as possible." Hard deadlinesTrade show, grand opening, end of quarter, funding milestone. Budget rangeA bracket is fine. Helps us scope features in or out. Ongoing support expectationsHands-off after launch, monthly care plan, or somewhere in between? Who signs off?The decision-makers on your side and the order they need to approve.
