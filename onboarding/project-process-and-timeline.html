Client Onboarding

Project Process & Timeline

Every project moves through the same six steps. Each step has a clear purpose, a specific thing I need from you, and a single milestone that says it's done. No surprises.

1 Getting started

Typical duration: 1 week

I gather everything I need to start: your goals, your customers, your brand, your content, and when you want to be live. This step exists so I never design blind.

What happens

  • Kickoff call to walk through your intake questionnaire.
  • A list of your pages and features, drawn up and reviewed together.
  • A login created for your dashboard so you can follow progress.

What I need from you

  • The filled-out Client Intake Questionnaire.
  • Brand bits (logo, colors, photos) in whatever form you have.
  • Notes on your current web address and anything you want kept (Google Business listing, traffic reports, etc.).
MilestoneSigned plan & page list.

2 Design

Typical duration: 1–2 weeks

I turn the plan into a real look — fonts, colors, layout, the way things move. You'll see real designs of your own pages, not a stock template with your name dropped in.

What happens

  • The look and feel proposed: fonts, color use, layout tone.
  • Full pictures of your homepage and one or two main inside pages.
  • One organized round of changes, then your sign-off.

What I need from you

  • Clear feedback all in one pass — not little notes trickled in over days.
  • The people who actually make the call looking at the designs, not just hearing about them.
MilestoneDesign signed off.

3 Build

Typical duration: 2–4 weeks

The approved design becomes a real, quick-loading website. I build it from the ground up so the result is fast, easy for everyone to use, and easy to update later.

What happens

  • Each page built and checked against the design.
  • Your words, photos, and details added.
  • Contact forms wired up so messages reach the right inbox.
  • A private preview link shared so you can click through real pages.

What I need from you

  • Final wording and any photos still outstanding.
  • Answers on anything I flagged during Design.
MilestonePreview site ready to click through.

4 Review & checks

Typical duration: 1 week

I make sure the site works everywhere it needs to — and that nothing slipped between design and build.

What happens

  • Tested on phones, tablets, and computers across all the main browsers.
  • A pass to make sure people with disabilities can use the site — keyboard navigation, color contrast, clear labels.
  • A speed pass: properly sized photos and a check of how quickly each page opens.
  • A read-through for typos, broken links, and missing photo descriptions.

What I need from you

  • Fresh eyes on the preview site from someone who wasn't deep in the project.
  • One combined list of any final fixes you want before launch.
MilestoneFinal fixes done, launch approved.

5 Launch

Typical duration: 1–2 days

I point your domain at the new site, verify everything is working, and put the portal into post-launch mode.

What happens

  • DNS cutover at a scheduled time we agree on together in advance.
  • Email and existing DNS records confirmed intact.
  • Analytics, Search Console, and uptime monitoring confirmed live.
  • Final review of the live URL on real devices.

What I need from you

  • Access to your domain registrar (or someone on standby who has it).
  • An hour of availability during the planned cutover window.
MilestoneSite live at your domain.

6 Ongoing Care

Continuous

Launch is the start, not the end. The portal keeps watching, I keep iterating, and you get a clear lane for changes.

What happens

  • Uptime, visitor counts, and page views streamed into your portal in real time.
  • Security and dependency updates handled by me, not you.
  • Scheduled check-ins to review performance and prioritize improvements.
  • Change requests handled through the agreed lane (see Communication & Support).

What I need from you

  • A single point of contact for change requests.
  • Honest feedback when something isn't working — sooner is always better.
MilestoneHealthy site, visible metrics, no surprises.
