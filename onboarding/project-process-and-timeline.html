Project Process & Timeline

Every TaylorURL build moves through the same six phases. Each phase has a clear purpose, a specific ask from you, and a single milestone that signals it's done. No surprises.

1 Discovery & Intake

Typical duration: 1 week

I collect everything I need to start: business goals, audience, brand assets, content sources, target launch date. This phase exists so I never design in the dark.

What happens

  • Kickoff call to walk through your completed intake questionnaire.
  • Sitemap and feature list drafted and reviewed together.
  • Account created in the TaylorURL client portal so you can follow progress.

What I need from you

  • The completed Client Intake Questionnaire.
  • Brand assets (logo, colors, photography) in any form you have them.
  • Access notes for your existing domain and any analytics you want preserved.
MilestoneSigned scope & sitemap.

2 Design

Typical duration: 1–2 weeks

We translate the scope into a visual direction — typography, color, layout, motion — then design the key page templates. You'll see real designs, not generic templates.

What happens

  • Visual direction proposed: typography, color usage, layout tone.
  • High-fidelity designs of the homepage and 1–2 key interior templates.
  • One structured round of revisions, then sign-off.

What I need from you

  • Clear feedback in one consolidated pass — not drip notes over days.
  • Decision-makers actually looking at the work, not just relaying opinions.
MilestoneApproved visual design.

3 Build

Typical duration: 2–4 weeks

The approved design is implemented as a real, performant React site. We build with React 19, Vite, and Tailwind so the result is fast, accessible, and easy to extend.

What happens

  • Page templates implemented in code and reviewed against design.
  • Content loaded — copy, images, structured data.
  • Forms wired up to the destinations agreed in Discovery.
  • Staging preview link shared so you can click through real pages.

What I need from you

  • Final copy and any outstanding assets, locked in.
  • Decisions on anything we flagged during Design that's still open.
MilestoneStaging site click-through ready.

4 Review & QA

Typical duration: 1 week

We make sure the site works everywhere it needs to — and that nothing slipped between design and build.

What happens

  • Cross-device and cross-browser checks (desktop, tablet, mobile).
  • Accessibility pass: keyboard navigation, contrast, labels, focus order.
  • Performance pass: image sizing, lazy loading, Lighthouse-level audits.
  • Content proof: typos, broken links, missing alt text.

What I need from you

  • A walkthrough of the staging site from people who weren't deep in the project.
  • A single consolidated punch list of any final corrections.
MilestonePunch list cleared, launch approved.

5 Launch

Typical duration: 1–2 days

We point your domain at the new site, verify everything is working, and put the portal into post-launch mode.

What happens

  • DNS cutover at a scheduled time we agree on in advance.
  • Email and existing DNS records confirmed intact.
  • Analytics, Search Console, and uptime monitoring confirmed live.
  • Final review of the live URL on real devices.

What I need from you

  • Access to your domain registrar (or someone on standby who has it).
  • An hour of availability during the planned cutover window.
MilestoneSite live at your domain.

6 Ongoing Care

Continuous

Launch is the start, not the end. The portal keeps watching, we keep iterating, and you get a clear lane for changes.

What happens

  • Uptime, visitor counts, and page views streamed into your portal in real time.
  • Security and dependency updates handled by us, not you.
  • Scheduled check-ins to review performance and prioritize improvements.
  • Change requests handled through the agreed lane (see Communication & Support).

What I need from you

  • A single point of contact for change requests.
  • Honest feedback when something isn't working — sooner is always better.
MilestoneHealthy site, visible metrics, no surprises.
