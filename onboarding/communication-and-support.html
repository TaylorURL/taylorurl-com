Communication & Support

The smoothest projects are the ones where everyone knows where to send what, how soon they'll hear back, and what counts as in-scope. Here's how I run it.

Day-to-day

Email

The default for non-urgent project work — questions, content, decisions, approvals. Replies are written and searchable.

Same-day

SMS / Phone

For time-sensitive items during business hours, or when something needs a quick yes/no to keep the project moving.

Inside the work

Your dashboard

Documents, drafts, health, and ongoing numbers live in the dashboard — not buried in an email thread.

Urgent

Site is down

3 Requesting changes & reporting issues

Whether it's a typo or a brand-new section, send it the same way. I'll triage and respond.

What to include

  • What you want changed. Plain English is fine.
  • Where it is. A page URL or screenshot saves me 80% of the back-and-forth.
  • Why it matters & how urgent. Helps me prioritize honestly.
  • Anything time-sensitive. A campaign, an event, a deadline.

What happens next

  1. I confirm I got it within the times above.
  2. For anything beyond a small tweak, I send a quick plan and price before starting.
  3. The change lands on a private preview first whenever possible so you can look it over before it goes live.

4 What's included, what's extra

Being clear about what is in and what is extra protects both of us. I'm direct about both, and about how I move things between the two.

Included

  • Anything spelled out in your signed plan.
  • Small content edits while we're building — wording tweaks, photo swaps, fixing broken links.
  • Fixing anything I built that isn't working right.

Extra

  • New pages, new features, or hooking the site up to new outside services that weren't in the original plan.
  • Redoing the design after you've signed off on it.
  • Work on systems I don't already look after for you.

How extra changes work

  1. You send the request through any of the channels above.
  2. I let you know whether it's included, and if not, a clear price.
  3. If you give the green light, it joins the schedule and I get moving.
  4. No surprise invoices — extra work only happens after you say yes.

5 Billing

Clear, predictable invoices. No mystery line items.

For website builds

  • A deposit at kickoff to hold your spot and start the project.
  • A second invoice when you sign off on the design.
  • Final invoice at launch, due before the site goes live.

For ongoing care

  • Monthly invoice on the same date each month, agreed at the start of the care plan.
  • Any approved extra work shows up on the next monthly invoice.
  • Pause or stop the care plan with 30 days' notice — no long-term lock-in.

How to pay

  • Card or bank transfer through a secure invoice link.
  • Receipts and past invoices live in your dashboard so you can grab them any time.

6 Short FAQ

Who owns the site after launch?

You do. The code, the content, and the domain are yours. I'll happily keep operating it for you, but nothing locks you in.

What if my site goes down at 2 AM?

Monitoring pages me automatically. In most cases I'm already investigating before you wake up. You can text the urgent line at any hour.

Can my team help update content directly?

Yes — depending on what you want to control, I'll either give you a content editor or handle updates for you within your care plan. I'll recommend the right setup at launch.

How do you handle requests during a heavy week?

Honestly. If something will land later than the standard target, I tell you when to expect it. I'd rather set a real date than miss an optimistic one.

What if I'm not sure whether something is in scope?

Just ask. I'll never charge for asking, and I'll always give you a straight answer about whether it counts as a change request.
