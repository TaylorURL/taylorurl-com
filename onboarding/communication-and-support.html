Communication & Support

The smoothest projects are the ones where everyone knows where to send what, how soon they'll hear back, and what counts as in-scope. Here's how I run it.

1 How to reach me

One channel per kind of message keeps things from getting lost. Pick the right lane and your request gets to the right place.

Day-to-day

Email

The default for non-urgent project work — questions, content, decisions, approvals. Replies are written and searchable.

Same-day

SMS / Phone

For time-sensitive items during business hours, or when something needs a quick yes/no to keep the project moving.

Inside the work

Client portal

Document delivery, deliverables, status, and ongoing metrics live in the portal — not buried in an email thread.

Urgent

Site down / outage

Call or text — and I'm already on it. Outage alerts page me automatically, but a direct heads-up is always welcome.

3 Requesting changes & reporting issues

Whether it's a typo or a brand-new section, send it the same way. I'll triage and respond.

What to include

  • What you want changed. Plain English is fine.
  • Where it is. A page URL or screenshot saves me 80% of the back-and-forth.
  • Why it matters & how urgent. Helps me prioritize honestly.
  • Anything time-sensitive. A campaign, an event, a deadline.

What happens next

  1. I confirm receipt within the targets above.
  2. For anything beyond a small tweak, I send a quick scope and estimate before starting.
  3. Work lands on staging first whenever possible so you can review it before it goes live.

4 Scope & change requests

Scope clarity protects both sides. I'm direct about what's in, what's out, and how I move things from one column to the other.

In-scope changes

  • Anything explicitly listed in your signed scope.
  • Small content edits during the active engagement — copy tweaks, image swaps, link fixes.
  • Bug fixes on work I've delivered.

Out-of-scope changes

  • New pages, new features, or new integrations not in the original scope.
  • Redesigns or visual reworks after design sign-off.
  • Work on systems I don't already manage for you.

How change requests work

  1. You send the request through any of the channels above.
  2. I respond with whether it's in scope, and if not, a clear estimate.
  3. If you approve the estimate, it joins the schedule and I get moving.
  4. No surprise invoices — work outside scope only happens after you've said yes.

5 Billing cadence

Clear, predictable billing. No mystery line items.

For build engagements

  • A deposit at kickoff to confirm the schedule and start Discovery.
  • A progress invoice at the design sign-off milestone.
  • Final invoice at launch, due before the public DNS cutover.

For ongoing care

  • Monthly invoice on a fixed date, agreed at the start of the care plan.
  • Any approved out-of-scope work bills on the next monthly invoice.
  • Pause or end the care plan with 30 days' notice — no long-term lock-in.

Methods

  • Card or ACH via secure invoice link.
  • Receipts and prior invoices live in the portal so you can grab them any time.

6 Short FAQ

Who owns the site after launch?

You do. The code, the content, and the domain are yours. I'll happily keep operating it for you, but nothing locks you in.

What if my site goes down at 2 AM?

Monitoring pages me automatically. In most cases I'm already investigating before you wake up. You can text the urgent line at any hour.

Can my team help update content directly?

Yes — depending on what you want to control, I'll either give you a content editor or handle updates for you within your care plan. I'll recommend the right setup at launch.

How do you handle requests during a heavy week?

Honestly. If something will land later than the standard target, I tell you when to expect it. I'd rather set a real date than miss an optimistic one.

What if I'm not sure whether something is in scope?

Just ask. I'll never charge for asking, and I'll always give you a straight answer about whether it counts as a change request.
